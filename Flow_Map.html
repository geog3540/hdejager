South America Banana Trade 2010

Image Highlight on Hover
Click on image to see JSON file

This map represents the tons of banana trades in South America in 2019. The countries that have exported more bananas than they have imported are indicated in light green, and the countries that import more bananas than they export are indicated in dark green. I chose this color scheme because it uses one hue of green, making it easy to see the transition between rates of imports and exports within countries. I thought green would be an apporopriate color to use here because South America contains lots of rainforests and it matches well with the yellow flow lines. I used the default color black for the nodes that show gross total trade, both import and export, because it stands out well and is easy to interpret. The size of the circle indicates the amount of trades that occur in that particular country. Finally, the flow arrows indicate where bananas are being imported and exported. I chose to use a yellow to light green scheme here because it compliments the varying shades of green. Areas that are in yellow show where bananas are being exported, and areas in light green show where bananas are being imported. The size of the arrow indicates the total volume of banana trades. The map shows that there is a high demand for banana imports in the southern countries such as Chile and Argentina as they are less suitable for growing bananas. Countries that are closer to the equator such as Brazil, Peru, and Columbia have climates favored to banana production, making them the highest exporters of bananas.
